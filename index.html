Our Story

The first time I met Sarah was over email. I was on Thanksgiving break and she was busy starting her new job. Of course it happened to be at the same place that I work and so Sarah was already busy sending emails to people she hadn't met telling them they needed to finish such-and-such at work - I happened to be one of those people. Those of you that know Sarah will likely agree that she is one of the hardest workers you'll ever meet. But of course under all of that toughness she is also the most sincere, caring and beautiful people I've ever met.

So given Sarah's nature and fun-loving spirit it wasn't long before I was spending every hour I could with her. This lead to us both being at the office constantly followed by trips for late night dinners and catching games at Dave and Busters. Now through this whole time I'm thinking she is totally into me. I mean come-on, spending that much time together!? That had to be a sign right? Well she's going to tell you that she had know idea I liked her - even though I still don't believe that :)

Eventually she decided to give me a chance. She says it like this, "Well you grew on me...kinda like a fungus". So romantic! But given the months of effort it was the best thing I'd ever heard. A few years later we found ourselves on top of a small hilltop village in Italy named San Gimignano and a few hours after that she finally completely gave in. I will probably always give her a hard time about how hard it was for her to finally date me (and that she's a Niners fan), but I know the rest of our lives will be filled with laughter and love. I'm so happy I found my soulmate.

When I hear the buzz of the little world among the stalks, and grow familiar with the countless indescribable forms of the insects and flies, then I feel the presence of the Almighty, who formed us in his own image, and the breath.

Groomsmen

  • Justin Reynolds - Brother I'm not really sure you can sum up your relationship with your brother in a few lines. Actually in our case I know I can't. Let's see he almost shot my eye out with a bb-gun (he will tell you the story in a heartbeat if you haven't heard it), he was famous for driving my friends crazy (so much so that they rode bikes to our house just to kick his butt) and he will brag endlessly about how much better he is than me at video games (and fishing and shooting and...). Regardless we still find ourselves living in the same city and calling each other up to hang out all the time. I guess sometimes someone can drive you crazy enough that when they aren't around you miss it. I love you buddy.

  • Craig Brunner - College Buddy Mr. Craigles and I met our first year in college while we were dorm-mates at UPS. Since then we've spent countless hours mobbin nubes and drinking brews. He is the reason I moved to San Jose when we decided to start a certain website together (a few of you may know about that). Of course even though he is the reason that I am where I am today, he'll be the first person to tell you that while I'm on level one, he's already beat the game.

  • Litton Chen - San Jose Buddy Ever since Litton noticed a MySQL book on my desk and asked me to help build just an inventory management system, he's been giving me work to do. On a serious note though, Litton and I have become great friends. From countless Vegas trips to Thailand and Ireland we've done a lot the past few years and he is one of the reasons San Jose has become a home.

  • Ryan Wells - Hometown Buddy Ryan is one of the people in my life that no matter where I am or how long it's been since we've seen each other, I know that the moment we are together we will pick up where we last left off. I've known Ryan since I was in fourth grade. We played football together in High School and I would come home to watch his games at the University of Montana during college. I do credit him with playing at least a part in making me complain about my stomach for the rest of my life (not only does he do the same thing but we spent one school year eating nothing but burritos and chicken strips for lunch). Also there was a time when we could recite every single line of Nelly's album Country Grammar and the movie Dumb and Dumber. Chances are probably still can.

  • Todd Kenley - Hometown Buddy TK and I go waaaay back. Before I moved from MT to CA in 2nd grade, we would be at his house playing with Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles and finding spiders in his garage. Once I came back from CA we continued our friendship that lasts to this day. From starting our own wolf pack in High School (I'm talking the WCW here - real deal) to destroying anyone who plays us in Beer Pong we are a life-long team. You're the man TK!

  • Tom Campbell - College Buddy Tom and I become great friends through our time at Phi Delta Theta together. He was my roommate senior year and always the first guy to be there for me whenever I needed him. Tom is a person who is 100 percent true to himself and what he believes in. I've always looked up to him for his ability to make people laugh and relax. Oh and also whenever I find myself in a scotch bar in NYC he is the first one I call for advice.
